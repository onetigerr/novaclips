# YouTube Video Specification: "Why AI Forgets — Tokens & Context Window Explained"

## Project Overview

**Format:** Educational video essay  
**Duration:** 10 minutes (~1500 words spoken)  
**Target Audience:** Ages 14–25 + curious adults; non-technical ChatGPT users  
**Topic:** How tokens and context windows work in LLMs (main insight: context = input + output tokens, a real computational constraint)  
**Genre:** Educational storytelling / Video essay  
**Tone:** Clear, plain-spoken, relatable; no jargon; uses everyday analogies

---

## Visual Design Philosophy

### Atmosphere: "Tidy Digital Workshop"

A space where high-level technical concepts are dismantled and organized. The mood is **lucid, grounded, and rhythmic**, avoiding "hacker" tropes in favor of a sophisticated, painterly aesthetic.

**Core Principles:**
- **Tactile Logic** — AI constraints visualized as physical, tangible objects (blocks, whiteboards, backpacks) to demystify "digital amnesia"
- **Painterly Depth** — Using *Arcane* aesthetic; environment feels "lived-in" and hand-crafted, moving from dark moody corners to vibrant illuminated data centers
- **Intellectual Clarity** — Visual flow transitions from "clutter" of overwhelming data to "structure" of the Architect's toolkit

### Scene Distribution: Real Life + Tech Visualization

**Balance:** 60-70% real-life scenes + 30-40% tech visualizations

**Real-Life Environments (60-70% of scenes):**
- Coffee shops and cafés
- Urban outdoor locations (parks, streets, rooftops)
- Restaurants and casual dining spaces
- Modern offices and coworking spaces
- Retail stores and shopping environments
- Libraries and study spaces
- Home interiors (living rooms, workspaces)
- Public transportation (trains, buses)
- Gyms and fitness centers
- Creative studios and workshops

**Tech Visualization Scenes (30-40% of scenes):**
- Abstract token visualizations
- Server racks and data centers
- Isometric diagrams and schematics
- UI mockups and interfaces
- Metaphorical representations (whiteboards, backpacks, scales)

**Visual Unity:**
- All scenes rendered in consistent **Arcane-painterly style**
- Hand-painted digital textures with visible brushstrokes apply to both real-life and tech scenes
- Ink-trace shadows and volumetric lighting unified across all environments
- Natural, diverse color palettes for real-life scenes while maintaining brand accent colors (Electric Cyan, Soft Amber) in tech elements
- Dramatic lighting creates cohesive cinematic atmosphere regardless of setting

**Color Approach for Real-Life Scenes:**
- Natural, warm tones for cafés and restaurants (wood browns, cream whites, warm lighting)
- Cool urban tones for outdoor city scenes (concrete grays, sky blues, natural daylight)
- Vibrant retail colors for shopping environments (product displays, signage)
- Muted professional tones for offices (neutral grays, soft blues, clean whites)
- Brand accent colors (Electric Cyan, Soft Amber) integrated through glowing tech devices, holographic overlays, AR interfaces within real environments

**Environment Diversity Benefits:**
- Makes technical concepts more relatable and grounded
- Provides visual variety and maintains viewer engagement
- Shows how AI interactions happen in everyday contexts
- Balances abstract tech concepts with tangible human experiences
- Creates opportunities for natural metaphors (café conversations for context, shopping choices for token budgets)

## Animation Approach

### Primary Technique: Ken Burns Effect

**What It Is:**
- 90% of animation = slow camera movement on static images
- Techniques: gradual zoom in, zoom out, slow pan left-to-right
- Purpose: creates motion illusion, prevents "dead" static feeling

**Implementation:**
- Apply to all concept illustrations, metaphor visualizations, AND real-life scenes
- Speed: very slow (3-5 second moves)
- Can be done in CapCut, Premiere, or any basic editor
- Consistent 45-degree isometric perspective maintained for tech scenes
- Cinematic camera angles for real-life scenes (wide, medium, close-up)

**Camera Movements:**
- Slow isometric pans across tech objects
- Cinematic pans across real-life environments
- Gentle zoom on key elements
- Smooth tracking following visual flow
- Minimal rotation around focal points

### Transitions

**Ink/Smoke Transitions:**
- Only complex graphic element needed
- Use pre-made overlay footage with alpha channel
- White/black smoke "wipes" between major sections
- Creates cohesive flow between real-life and tech scenes
- Apply at section changes (Hook→Map, Map→Content, between key points)

**Standard Transitions:**
- Fade for subtle shifts within sections
- Typography fade-in synchronized with narration
- Smooth dissolve between real-life and tech visualization scenes
- No jarring cuts

### Object Animation

**Minimal Animation Required:**
- Token blocks: simple glow pulsing, stacking effects, overflow
- UI elements: fade in/out, progress bars filling
- Whiteboard/surfaces: text appearing, erasing
- Backpack/containers: blocks flowing in, capacity meter filling
- Server racks/activity indicators: lights intensifying
- Real-life elements: subtle ambient motion (steam from coffee, passing pedestrians, screen glow)
- No character animation needed (5-6 shots with minimal movement)

**Focus:**
- Strong composition + dramatic lighting (both real and tech scenes)
- Hand-painted texture quality maintained across all environments
- Volumetric lighting effects unified throughout
- Visible brushstrokes maintained in both real-life and tech scenes

- Minimal animation skills required—basic video editor sufficient

- Scalable workflow—can upgrade visual quality without changing structure
- AI-generated art maintains consistent Arcane painterly style across diverse environments
- Single narrator, no extensive on-camera presence needed
- Combination of real-life and tech scenes provides natural visual variety

**Philosophy:**

## Production Workflow

### Pre-Production
1. **Script finalization** — read narration aloud, ensure natural flow, timing matches sections
2. **Visual research** — collect reference images for both tech metaphors AND real-life environments in Arcane style
3. **Scene distribution planning** — map out 60-70% real-life vs 30-40% tech visualization balance across timeline
4. **Storyboard** — separate document for detailed shot list
5. **Style consistency check** — test generate 2-3 tech assets AND 2-3 real-life environment assets with style token to verify consistency

### Production
1. **Voice recording** — record full narration first (easier to edit visuals to audio timing)
2. **Asset creation batch** — generate all illustrations (tech AND real-life) using Arcane painterly style token
3. **Base animation** — apply Ken Burns effects to each image (2-4 seconds per shot), both scene types
4. **Timeline assembly** — build video timeline in editor synchronized to voice-over, maintaining 60-70% real-life balance

### Post-Production
1. **Transitions** — add smoke/ink overlays at major section changes (work seamlessly between scene types)
2. **Typography** — animate subtitles and key term appearances
3. **Texture pass** — apply overlay textures for cohesive Arcane painterly look across all scenes
4. **Sound design** — layer ambient sounds appropriate to each scene type (café sounds, office sounds, tech hums, UI effects)
5. **Color grading** — ensure consistent painterly aesthetic, proper contrast, natural colors for real-life scenes with brand accent integration
6. **Final mix** — balance voice, music, and effects levels
7. **Review pass** — check pacing, clarity, engagement flow, scene distribution balance

## Production Notes & Tips

### Budget-Friendly Alternatives
- **No After Effects needed** — CapCut or Premiere sufficient for MVP
- **AI-generated art** — consistent Arcane painterly style applied to both tech and real-life scenes
- **Royalty-free assets** — smoke transitions available on free sites
- **Single narrator** — no need for multiple voices
- **Minimal on-camera** — character appearances limited to conceptual shots

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- **Don't over-animate** — focus on strong static composition for both scene types
- **Don't sacrifice style consistency** — real-life scenes must maintain Arcane painterly aesthetic
- **Don't rush voice-over** — pacing is critical for comprehension
- **Don't use too many fonts** — stick to 1-2 maximum
- **Don't overuse transitions** — smoke effects only at major section breaks
- **Don't forget sound design** — ambient audio appropriate to each environment makes scenes feel alive
- **Don't neglect scene balance** — maintain 60-70% real-life distribution throughout video
